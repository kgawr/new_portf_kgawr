Revenue by Category

Q: Which product categories generate the most revenue? · SQL: GROUP BY, SUM, JOIN

Key finding: Electronics dominates revenue despite being mid-range in order volume, driven by high unit prices. Books have the highest order count but lowest revenue per order.

Monthly Revenue Trend

Q: How did sales evolve over 2 years? · SQL: SUBSTR for date grouping, dual-axis

Key finding: Revenue and order volume track closely. Q4 spikes visible in both years, consistent with holiday shopping. Month-over-month growth was ~8% on average.

Return Rate by Category

Q: Which categories have the highest return rates? · SQL: CASE WHEN, conditional COUNT

Key finding: Clothing and Electronics have the highest return rates — consistent with industry benchmarks. Books and Sports have the lowest. Targeting high-return categories for quality improvements could reduce operational costs.

Revenue by Customer Segment

Q: How do customer segments compare? · SQL: CTE (WITH), nested aggregation

Key finding: VIP customers represent ~10% of the base but generate disproportionate revenue. Regular customers form the stable foundation. New customers show potential for upsell.

Top Products per Category

Q: Which products lead in each category? · SQL: RANK() OVER (PARTITION BY category)

Key finding: In Electronics, Laptops and Smartphones dominate — top 2 products account for ~40% of category revenue. Window functions make this ranking efficient and scalable across any number of categories.
